Selected Projects

Below is a short selection of projects that show how I approach problems—start with the simplest useful version, add measurement, then iterate. Most of these began as small prototypes and grew as I validated impact. I prefer writing clear READMEs, keeping dependency lists minimal, and publishing reproducible notebooks or scripts. When relevant, I document failure modes and include example prompts, inputs, and outputs so others can evaluate behavior quickly.

For retrieval‑augmented generation, I’ve built compact pipelines that treat data quality as a first‑class constraint. That includes content filters, chunking tuned to the query types, and caching layers that reduce cost without sacrificing freshness. I also created lightweight evaluators that catch hallucinations and formatting drift. In reinforcement learning, I’ve explored simple decision loops that combine reflection with guardrails, prioritizing traceability over raw complexity. Across all of this, my goal is to produce tools that teammates can understand and extend without friction.
